1. Who are considered vulnerable populations, and what challenges might they face in digital spaces?

Vulnerable groups are those with system barriers to society. They are:

- Neurodiverse populations (ADHD, Autism, Dyslexia, etc.)
- People with mental illnesses (anxiety, depression, PTSD, etc.)
- Those with physical or sensory impairments
- Those with low digital literacy or access to digital resources

Challenges in digital spaces for them:

- Too much information ‚Äì Too much to read or overly complicated interfaces obstruct users from being able to process.
- Unreliable navigation ‚Äì Intermittent UI fails most users, including those with ADHD.
- Sensory overload ‚Äì Jarring hues, flashing elements, or excessive notifications can be overwhelming.
- Time management challenges ‚Äì Rigorous timers or productivity applications that are not flexible can be stressful.

2. What ethical considerations are important when designing or working with neurodivergent individuals? (Hint: Avoid overwhelming UX, respect sensory needs, ensure clear communication.)

- **Don't overwhelm UX** ‚Äì Simplify and make interfaces predictable with clear labels.
- **Respect sensory needs** ‚Äì Permit customization of sound, visual, and notification.
- **Provide clear communication** ‚Äì Make language concise, direct, and positive.
- **Provide flexibility** ‚Äì Let users change productivity timers, task structure, or layout.

3. How can you make interactions and content more accessible for people with ADHD or Autism? (e.g., simple language, predictable navigation, reducing cognitive load)

- **Be simple** ‚Äì Avoid using jargon and maintain instructions simple.
- **Reduced cognitive load** ‚Äì Break up tasks into easy steps instead of complex instructions.
- **Meet different learning needs** ‚Äì Use visual, auditory, and text methods of instruction.
- **Allow customization** ‚Äì Allow users to customize settings according to their requirement for concentration.

4. How can we support neurodivergent team members in a professional setting? (Hint: Be clear in communication, respect different working styles, and allow flexibility.)

- **Be concise in communication** ‚Äì Don't give ambiguous instructions and provide step-by-step instructions.
- **Respect varying work styles** ‚Äì Some individuals require structured tasks, while others like flexible workflows.
- **Provide flexibility** ‚Äì Provide people with control over their schedules, breaks, and focus modes.
- **Check-in but don't micromanage** ‚Äì Give support, but provide autonomy.
